Problem Statement - Assisting Medical Experts in Timely Diagnosis of Polycystic Ovary Syndrom (PCOS)

Is It Really a Problem?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome, a.k.a. PCOS is a a hormonal disorder that ideally occurs in women in their reproductive stage. It affects aroung 116 million women worldwide with an average of 276.4 cases per 1,00,000 people in Europe alone! And many of them even go undiagnosed.

Being a problem in women's reproductive health, there is not much research on it. Resultantly it is neglected.

It is also falsely diagnosed many times negatively affecting already healthy women with a normally functioning reproductive system.

It can cause

  • diabetes
  • hyperplaxia
  • endometrial cancer (insert img)
  • insulin resistance
  • high bp
  • high cholesterol

Challenges

There are few but significant challenges as little research has been done.

  1. Difficult to obtain custome datasets due to patients not consenting to sharing their scans.
  2. Difficult to diagnose women in their menarcheal periods and menopausal periods

Impact in Medical Sectors

Our plan is to offer this program to hospitals locally and take their feedback for improvement. Being a one of its kind, we offer an immediate PCOS diagnosis like never before increasing its market value significantly.

Furthermore, providing our program to established hospitals at a fair price will also lead to more efforts in research for its treatment as diagnosis will be instantaneous and more time will be dedicated to curing the patients.

Future Scope

According to the Rotterdam Criteria, there are three conditions one must meet to solidify the diagnosis of PCOS. You must meet at least 2 out of these 3 conditions.They are:

  • Oligo/Amenorrhea
  • Androgen excess
  • Polycystic Ovarian Morphology
We will add two more algorithms to help diagnose these two criteria as well. Although a blood test is the current method to diagnose androgen excess, we aim to conveniently check all three conditions and have all information in one application for the convenience of medical experts and/or consultants.

An additional feature will also be provided where doctors(with the consent of the patient) can document the records of PCOS positive patients and send them to medical institutions to be further researched upon.
